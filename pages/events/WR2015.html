Abhayagiri 2015 Winter Retreat (partial) – The Four Foundations of Mindfulness
Ajahn Pasanno, Ajahn Karuṇadhammo and Ajahn Ñāniko
Abhayagiri Winter Retreat, Jan. 6, 2015 to Mar. 29, 2015
Abhayagiri Buddhist Monastery in Redwood Valley, California
5 sessions, 8 excerpts, 2:17:57 total duration

Ajahn Pasanno, Ajahn Karuṇadhammo, and Ajahn Ñāṇiko took turns read suttas and teachings regarding the Four Foundations of Mindfulness and answer questions on the subject. The slideshow illustrating the 32 parts of the body is particularly noteworthy.

Session 1: Broadly Established Mindfulness – Ajahn Pasanno – Jan. 6, 2015

Download audio (24:33)
Dhamma talk: After the Abhayagiri community begins the retreat with the ceremony of taking dependence, Ajahn Pasanno explains the meaning of dependence, the importance of mindfulness, and how mindfulness connects with the eightfold path. He explains how to look after both oneself and others with mindfulness using the Simile of the Acrobat (SN 47.19). [Ceremony/ritual] [Dependence] [Mindfulness] [Right Mindfulness] [Eightfold Path]

Session 2: Right Effort and Mindfulness – Ajahn Pasanno – Jan. 7, 2015

Download audio (39:45)
Dhamma talk: Ajahn Pasanno reflects upon the question: “What kind of effort do we need to conform with what the Buddha means by mindfulness?” He answers in terms of the four aspects of right effort, the seven qualities of Dhamma the Buddha taught to Upali (AN 7.79), and the Buddha's description of how he crossed the flood (SN 1.1). [Right Effort] [Mindfulness] [Dhamma]

Session 3: Mindfulness and Clear Comprehension – Ajahn Pasanno – Jan. 8, 2015

Download audio (33:18)
Dhamma talk: The commentary explains sampajañña (clear comprehension / alertness) as clear comprehension of purpose, suitability, domain, and non-delusion. Ajahn Pasanno describes how each of these factors relate to the practice of mindfulness. [Clear comprehension] [Mindfulness]

Session 4: Ardent, Alert, and Mindful – Ajahn Pasanno – Jan. 9, 2015

Download audio (29:18)
Dhamma talk: Cautioning against trusting our assumptions about the nature of mindfulness, Ajahn Pasanno reviews several key passages in the Satipaṭṭhāna Sutta (MN 10). [Ardency] [Clear comprehension] [Mindfulness] [Right Mindfulness]
